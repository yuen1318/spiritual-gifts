1. Word of Wisdom
What is it?

The ability to apply God’s truth wisely in real-life situations.

Not just intelligence, but Spirit-led judgment.

New Testament Example

Stephen was full of wisdom and the Holy Spirit:

“They could not withstand the wisdom and the Spirit with which he spoke.” (Acts 6:10)

Today’s Example

A friend is confused about a serious moral decision.

Instead of arguing, you calmly help her see God’s truth with love and clarity.

That is wisdom in action.

2. Word of Knowledge
What is it?

A Spirit-given insight into a person or situation that you could not know naturally.

New Testament Example

Peter knew that Ananias and Sapphira were lying about their offering (Acts 5:1–11).

Today’s Example

While praying for someone, you suddenly sense they are struggling with guilt.

You gently bring it up, and they open their heart.

3. Faith
What is it?

A special confidence in God’s power beyond normal human trust.

New Testament Example

Peter said to the lame man:

“In the name of Jesus Christ, walk.” (Acts 3:6)

Today’s Example

Even in a difficult crisis, you remain deeply confident that God will provide.

Your peace strengthens others.

4. Gifts of Healing
What is it?

God working through a person to bring physical, emotional, or spiritual healing.

New Testament Example

Peter healed a lame man at the temple gate (Acts 3:1–10).

Today’s Example

You pray for someone sick or brokenhearted.

Healing may come physically, emotionally, or spiritually through God’s grace.

5. Miracles (Mighty Deeds)
What is it?

God doing something beyond natural explanation.

New Testament Example

Paul performed extraordinary miracles through God’s power (Acts 19:11–12).

Today’s Example

An impossible situation suddenly changes through prayer and God’s intervention.

6. Prophecy
What is it?

Speaking a message inspired by God to encourage, correct, or strengthen others.

(Not mainly about predicting the future.)

New Testament Example

Agabus predicted a famine that affected the Church (Acts 11:27–28).

Today’s Example

During prayer, you feel led to encourage someone:

“God has not forgotten you. Keep trusting Him.”

7. Discernment of Spirits
What is it?

The ability to recognize whether something comes from God, human influence, or evil.

New Testament Example

Paul discerned that the spirit in the slave girl was not from God (Acts 16:16–18).

Today’s Example

You evaluate teachings or movements carefully to see if they align with Scripture and Church teaching.

8. Tongues
What is it?

Prayer or speech inspired by the Holy Spirit in a language not naturally known by the speaker.

New Testament Example

At Pentecost, the disciples spoke in tongues and people understood them in their own languages (Acts 2:1–11).

Today’s Example

In prayer, some people experience praying in tongues as a form of deep spiritual worship.

9. Interpretation of Tongues
What is it?

The ability to understand and explain messages given in tongues.

New Testament Example

St. Paul teaches that tongues in public worship should be interpreted so the Church is built up (1 Corinthians 14).

Today’s Example

Someone interprets a message in tongues that brings encouragement to the community.

10. Service
Example

Stephen served the early Church by helping widows (Acts 6:1–6).

Today

Helping in church ministries, organizing, serving quietly behind the scenes.

11. Teaching
Example

Apollos taught the Scriptures accurately and powerfully (Acts 18:24–28).

Today

Leading Bible studies, catechism classes, or youth formation.

12. Encouragement (Exhortation)
Example

Barnabas encouraged and strengthened believers. His name means “Son of Encouragement.”

Today

Helping someone who is discouraged to continue following Jesus.

13. Giving
Example

Early Christians shared their possessions generously (Acts 4:32–35).

Today

Supporting the poor, missions, and church needs.

14. Leadership
Example

James helped lead the early Church during major decisions (Acts 15).

Today

Leading ministries, prayer groups, or church activities.

15. Mercy
Example

Jesus’ parable of the Good Samaritan (Luke 10:25–37).

Today

Helping the sick, the poor, the lonely, and those in crisis with compassion.
